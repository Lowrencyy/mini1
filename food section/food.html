Filipino cuisine is a rich and diverse culinary tradition that reflects the country's history, culture, and geography. It's characterized by a combination of indigenous ingredients, Spanish influence from colonial times, Chinese influences, and flavors unique to the Philippines. HOW WE COOK?

Cooking techniques and methods in Filipino cuisine are diverse, with various dishes prepared through a range of methods. Here are some common ways Filipinos cook. ADOBO

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its savory and slightly tangy flavor profile. It is considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines and is a staple in Filipino cuisine. FILIPINO SPECIALTIES

PANCIT CANTON

"Pancit" ay isang uri ng lutong pansit na kilala sa Pilipinas at kinakatawan ang kakaibang sarsa at lasa ng mga lutong ito. Karaniwang inihahanda ito sa mga okasyon at espesyal na pagdiriwang, at may iba't ibang uri at istilo ang pancit sa buong bansa. Ito ay isang paboritong pagkain ng mga Pilipino at may iba't ibang bersyon nito, tulad ng Pancit Canton, Pancit Malabon, Pancit Bihon, at marami pang iba.
