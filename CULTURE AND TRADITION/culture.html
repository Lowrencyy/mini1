WELCOME TO FILIPINO CULTURE

Filipino culture is a rich and diverse tapestry that has been shaped by centuries of history, colonization, trade, and migration. It is characterized by a unique blend of indigenous, Spanish, American, Chinese, Malay, and Islamic influences. Here are some key aspects of Filipino culture

Hospitality

Filipinos are known for their warm and welcoming hospitality. They often go out of their way to make guests feel at home and offer food and refreshments as a sign of respect and friendship.

FAMILY

EXTENDED FAMILY

An extended family is a family structure that goes beyond the nuclear family unit, which typically consists of parents and their children. In an extended family, multiple generations and various relatives live together or maintain close relationships, often in the same household or within close proximity. This family structure can vary greatly in terms of its composition and dynamics across different cultures and societies.

ORIENTED FAMILY

MAHINHIN

LARONG PINOY

"Mahinhin" is a Filipino word that can be translated to "reserved" or "modest" in English. It is used to describe someone who displays a demeanor of restraint, shyness, or modesty in their behavior, particularly in social interactions. A "mahinhin" person tends to be polite, modest, and may not be overly expressive or assertive in their actions or words. This term is often used to appreciate and acknowledge someone's polite and demure personality.
